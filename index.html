Axis 02 — Physician-scientist

Research that changes bedside practice

My research programme integrates clinical endocrinology with registry-based and genetic studies, focused on bone and mineral metabolism and endocrine emergencies — moving toward personalized care in metabolic bone disease.

Hypoparathyroidism & mineral metabolism

Randomized trial evidence and systematic reviews on thiazide therapy, hypercalciuria, and renal protection in chronic hypoparathyroidism; PTH/PTHrP signaling disorders.

Personalized osteoporosis therapy

Using bone turnover markers and BMI to individualize denosumab and zoledronic acid dosing intervals in postmenopausal women — published in Osteoporosis International and Bone.

Endocrine emergencies

Real-world cohorts on etomidate in severe hypercortisolism, therapeutic plasma exchange in refractory thyrotoxicosis, and the spectrum of endocrine emergencies in low-resource settings.

Normative data & rare disease

Age-specific reference data for Indian populations; genetic and registry studies of X-linked hypophosphatemia, McCune–Albright syndrome, and inherited vasopressin resistance.

Principal investigation

A randomized crossover trial in non-surgical hypoparathyroidism

DM thesis conducted at PGIMER: a placebo-controlled, single-blinded randomized crossover trial of hydrochlorothiazide with salt restriction (CTRI/2024/08/090041), published in JBMR Plus — among the few randomized trials worldwide addressing hypercalciuria in this rare disease.
